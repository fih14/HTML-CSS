Louis Pasteur

Benefactor of humanity

Louis Pasteur's life:

  • 1822 - Born in Dole, France.
  • 1845 - Receives the licencié ès sciences degree at the École Normale Supérieure in Paris, France.
  • 1848 - Resolves a problem concerning the nature of tartaric acid. (Molecular assymetry) He observes that, in racemic mixtures of tartrates, half of the crystals were right-handed (dextrorotatory) and half were left-handed (levorotatory).
  • 1858 - Demostrates, in alcoholic fermentation, that yeast was responsible for fermentation to produce alcohol from sugar.
  • 1861 - Observes that sugar fermented per part of yeast when the yeast was exposed to air. The lower rate of fermentation aerobically became known as the Pasteur effect.
  • 1862 - Disproves the the Doctrine of Spontaneous Generation. He placed boiled liquid in a flask and let hot air enter the flask. Then he closed the flask, and no organisms grew in it
  • 1865 - Inventes a process in which liquids are heated to a temperature between 60 and 100 °C. This killed most bacteria and moulds already present within them. The method became known as pasteurization, and was soon applied to beer and milk.
  • 1866 - Publishes Etudes sur le Vin, about the diseases of wine.
  • 1867 - Achieves an effective process to stop infections in Silkworm disease.
  • 1876 - Publishes Etudes sur la Bière, concerning the diseases of beer.
  • 1877 - First work on vaccine development was on chicken cholera. It was not regular in its effects and was a failure.
  • 1881 - Vaccination against anthrax. He had not used attenuation, but secretly used Toussaint's technique, and was criticized by Koch and other colleagues.
  • 1885 - Uses the vaccine for rabies on 9-year-old Joseph Meister after the boy was badly mauled by a rabid dog. Over 11 days, Meister received 13 inoculations, each inoculation using viruses that had been weakened for a shorter period of time.
  • 1888 - Institute Pasteur opens in Paris.
  • 1892 - Jubilee celebrations at the Soborne.
  • 1895 - Dies at the age of 72 in Villeneuve L'Étang, near Paris.
